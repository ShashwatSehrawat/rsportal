Department of Physics

The department offers a vibrant environment for post graduate and research. The Department proposes to carry out research in the areas of Condensed Matter Physics and High Energy Physics. In condensed matter Physics, magnetism, semiconductors and DFT methods for band structure studies are some of the areas of interest. Some of the areas of interest in High Energy Physics are B Physics, Neutrino Physics and Astro-particle Physics, Collider Phenomenology and perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics. The Department is currently offering Ph.D (Physics) in the areas mentioned above.
At the High Energy Physics group we are concerned with some of the fundamental mysteries about origins of matter, energy and space-time. The members of our group have focused their efforts on addressing some intriguing questions such as: What causes the observed overabundance of matter over antimatter, what is dark matter made up of, how do we identify or detect such dark matter particles, how do analytic properties of Feynman Integrals constrain their form at higher orders, can we discover the all order infrared structure of scattering amplitudes of quarks and gluons, how does a quantum space-time emerge, how do singularities such as in black holes and big bang get resolved, can computational complexity/quantum information teach us new things about quantum gravity. The HEP group is also a part of the NOvA and DUNE Neutrino collaboration at Fermilab, USA; Belle collaboration at KEK, JAPAN.
At the Astrophysics group we are mainly focusing on observational astrophysics and we do simulations and partially we are also doing cosmology upto certain extent. As long as experimental condensed matter is concerned we have listed following topics as an area of research Multifunctional oxides ,Colossal Magnetoresistive, Spintronic, Multiferroic and Masgnetocaloric Materials; Spin crossover Materials, Molecular bistability and Photomagnetism.
MEMS & MicroNano System Lab /Microelectronics technology, MEMS & MicroNano System Lab based bio/chemical and mechanical sensors, Polymer MEMS & MicroNano System Lab , RFMEMS & MicroNano System Lab , Thin films for MEMS & MicroNano System Lab , In-situ observation of semiconductor processes. Nanomagnetism, Data Storage, Scanning Probe and X-ray based magnetic imaging, Ferromagnetic semiconductor, Spintronics, Resonant Magnetic scattering, Ultra-fast magnetism and Micromagnetic Simulations.
Study of magnetic materials and devices at low temparatures and high magnetic fields, magnetostrictive materials, magnetic nanoparticles/multilayers, mesoscopic physics, solar cells, magnetism in oxides. Magnon spintronics, Nanomagnetic devices, Imaging spin waves at the nanoscale using Brillouin light scattering spectro-microscopy, Ferromagnetic resonance, Thin film nanofabrication (Lithography), Functional magnetic materials.
